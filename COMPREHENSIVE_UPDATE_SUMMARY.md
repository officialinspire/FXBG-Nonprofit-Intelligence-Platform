## üÜï NEW Organizations Added (12)

### **Youth Programs (3)**

1. **Big Brothers Big Sisters Greater Fredericksburg**
   - Type: Youth mentoring
   - Budget: ~$400K annually
   - Mission: One-to-one mentoring relationships for youth
   - Key Programs: Bigs in Schools, Bowl for Kids' Sake fundraiser
   - Serves: 5-county region (Fredericksburg, Stafford, King George, Caroline, Spotsylvania)

2. **Boys & Girls Club of the Rappahannock Region**
   - Type: Youth development
   - Budget: ~$1M+ annually
   - Mission: Building confidence and character in youth
   - Key Programs: After-school programs for 2,500+ kids, sports, arts, academics
   - Founded: 2001 (renamed from Fredericksburg Regional BGC in 2013)

3. **Stafford Junction**
   - Type: Youth education & family support
   - Budget: ~$500K annually
   - Mission: Education, empowerment, healthy living for families in poverty
   - Key Programs: Brain Builders tutoring, Camp Junction, food assistance
   - Founded: 2002 by Stafford County Sheriff's Department

### **Housing & Homelessness (2)**

4. **Thurman Brisben Center**
   - Type: Emergency homeless shelter
   - Budget: ~$2M+ annually
   - Mission: 24/7/365 emergency shelter and transformation services
   - Key Programs: Region's largest congregate shelter, wraparound services
   - Founded: 35+ years ago
   - Location: 471 Central Rd, Fredericksburg

5. **Loisann's Hope House**
   - Type: Family homeless shelter
   - Budget: ~$1.5M+ annually
   - Mission: Lead agency for housing crisis response
   - Key Programs: Emergency family shelter, prevention services, case management
   - Note: First and largest family homeless shelter in region

### **Seniors & Healthcare (3)**

6. **Healthy Generations Area Agency on Aging**
   - Type: Senior services
   - Budget: ~$3M+ annually
   - Mission: Enhancing quality of life for older citizens
   - Key Programs: Older Americans Act programs, Medicare counseling, VICAP
   - Serves: Planning District 16 (5 jurisdictions)
   - Languages: English, Spanish, French, Russian, Vietnamese

7. **Mental Health America Fredericksburg**
   - Type: Mental health
   - Budget: ~$300K annually
   - Mission: Promoting mental health through education and support
   - Key Programs: Senior Visitors Program, social connection programs

8. **ARC of the Rappahannock**
   - Type: Disability services
   - Budget: ~$1M+ annually
   - Mission: Care for individuals with intellectual/developmental disabilities
   - Key Programs: Daybreak day program, Saturday respite care

### **Environment & Education (2)**

9. **Downtown Greens**
   - Type: Urban agriculture & environment
   - Budget: ~$200K annually
   - Mission: Urban greenspace stewardship and environmental education
   - Key Programs: 2.8 acre greenspace, sustainability education, urban agriculture
   - Founded: 1995
   - Location: 206 Charles St, downtown Fredericksburg

10. **Central Rappahannock Regional Library**
    - Type: Education & community services
    - Budget: ~$8M+ annually (regional system)
    - Mission: Library services and educational programs
    - Key Programs: Multi-branch system, literacy programs, digital access

### **Employment & Emergency (2)**

11. **Rappahannock Goodwill Industries**
    - Type: Employment & workforce development
    - Budget: ~$10M+ annually (including retail)
    - Mission: Education, employment for people with barriers
    - Key Programs: Job training, workforce development, thrift retail operations
    - Model: Social enterprise
    - Partners with: Micah (income navigation), Empowerhouse (economic empowerment)

12. **Rappahannock Area Chapter American Red Cross**
    - Type: Emergency services
    - Budget: ~$1M+ annually
    - Mission: Disaster relief, blood services, preparedness training
    - Key Programs: Disaster response, CPR/first aid training, youth team
    - Phone: (540) 373-9807
